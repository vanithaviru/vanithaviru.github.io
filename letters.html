If you have previously taken my class and would like a letter of recommendation, please give me at least two weeks’ notice. If you give me less than two weeks’ notice, I will likely take pity on you and write you a letter anyway.

Class participation and attending office hours go a long way in allowing me to write you a strong letter of recommendation! (Coincidentally, they also help you get more out of my course.)

To request a letter:

Send me your unofficial transcript and resumé (both as PDFs), and answer the following questions.

  1. What was your prior experience with analytics, programming, or statistics?
  2. How has this class helped you grow? I mean this broadly — maybe this is learning a new skill, or maybe this is learning to push yourself to try new things.
  3. Do you anticipate using these skills in the future? If so, how?
  4. How did you study for this class? (TA office hours, meeting with friends, blocking off regular times, etc.)

Additionally, describe the program(s) you are applying to, and if you have a personal statement, send that as well. Whenever you get closer to the application process, send me a list of schools and their application deadlines — a Google spreadsheet works great for this.
